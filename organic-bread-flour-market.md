<p><p>The organic bread flour market is segmented primarily into machine milled flour and stone ground flour. Machine milled flour is produced using industrial mills that grind grain efficiently, resulting in a fine, uniform texture suitable for commercial baking. In contrast, stone ground flour involves traditional milling techniques with stones, preserving more nutrients and enhancing flavor, though it may have a coarser texture. Both types cater to health-conscious consumers seeking organic options but differ in processing methods and resultant baking qualities.</p></p>
