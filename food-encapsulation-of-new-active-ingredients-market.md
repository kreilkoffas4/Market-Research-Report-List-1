<p><p>The food encapsulation market for new active ingredients encompasses three main types: microencapsulation, nanoencapsulation, and hybrid encapsulation. Microencapsulation involves enclosing substances in micrometer-sized capsules, enhancing stability and controlled release. Nanoencapsulation, on the other hand, uses nanoparticles for more precise delivery and improved bioavailability of active ingredients. Hybrid encapsulation combines both micro and nano techniques to optimize benefits, such as increased efficiency and tailored release profiles, catering to diverse applications in food preservation, flavor enhancement, and nutritional delivery.</p></p>
